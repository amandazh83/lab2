Harry Potter, who was raised by his uncle's family, endured years of discrimination and bullying. However, on his 11th birthday, he discovered that he was, in fact, a wizard and had been accepted into Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry. Boarding the Hogwarts Express, Harry embarked on a magical journey full of wonder and surprises. At Hogwarts, he made his first true friends: Ron and Hermione. He also dove into fascinating magical subjects, including flying lessons, Defense Against the Dark Arts, Potions, and Transfiguration, not to mention the thrilling game of Quidditch that captivates every wizard.

On the other hand, Professor Snape, the Potions master, seemed to harbor an unfriendly attitude toward Harry. Meanwhile, Harry accidentally stumbled upon the secret of the Philosopher’s Stone. As dark schemes began to quietly grow in the seemingly peaceful halls of Hogwarts, Harry, Ron, and Hermione decided to investigate. With their courage and wit, Harry ultimately thwarted the evil plot and protected the Philosopher’s Stone. In the process, he also faced his arch-enemy, Voldemort, for the very first time.

Harry Potter was trapped at his aunt's house, but luckily, Ron came to his rescue just in time, allowing Harry to spend the rest of the summer happily at the Weasleys' home. However, the house-elf Dobby tried to warn Harry and used every means to prevent him from returning to school. When Harry and Ron were locked out of the platform, they had no choice but to fly a car back to Hogwarts.

The legendary Chamber of Secrets was opened by the heir of Slytherin, and Harry, due to his ability to speak Parseltongue, became the target of suspicion. Harry discovered a mysterious diary that contained Tom Riddle's memories from 50 years ago, and soon a series of terrifying attacks disrupted the once-peaceful campus. Ron's sister, Ginny, was taken into the Chamber, leaving a bloody warning on the walls. With Hogwarts facing the threat of closure, Harry and his friends had to act quickly to uncover the truth.

Harry Potter reluctantly spent his summer vacation at his aunt's house, completely cut off from the wizarding world. The letters from Ron and Hermione were his only solace. During a visit from Aunt Marge, Harry lost his temper after enduring her insults about his parents and accidentally inflated her like a giant balloon. Fearing punishment, Harry fled into the night, embarking on a new adventure.

In his panic, Harry saw a large black dog, a known omen of death in the wizarding world. On the train ride back to school, Harry encountered the legendary Dementors, which surrounded him with terrifying sensations. At school, he saw the ominous black dog again and soon learned the truth about his parents' death, blaming it all on Sirius Black. Determined to seek revenge, Harry resolved to confront him.

Professor Lupin taught Harry the magic to ward off Dementors, and with the help of the Marauder's Map, Harry finally found Sirius in the Shrieking Shack. Sirius pointed his wand at Ron's pet rat, Scabbers, and as Scabbers transformed into human form, Sirius revealed the truth about what had happened. Just as Sirius was about to clear his name, Snape intervened and ruined everything.

One summer night, Harry Potter was abruptly awakened by a sharp pain in his scar. He had a strange dream that left him deeply worried. Not long after, during the Quidditch World Cup, a terrifying event occurred—the Dark Mark of the evil wizard, who had disappeared thirteen years ago, appeared in the sky.

Hogwarts hosted a grand event in the wizarding world: the "Triwizard Tournament." Through dark magic, the evil wizard ensured that Harry, though underage, was chosen as the fourth competitor. With the help of his friends Hermione and Ron, Harry successfully completed the three tasks. However, when Harry and his fellow competitor Cedric lifted the Triwizard Cup in celebration, the nightmare had only just begun. The Cup was a Portkey that transported them to a graveyard. There, Cedric was killed by Wormtail, and Voldemort, who had been hidden in a snake-like form for thirteen years, was resurrected using Harry's blood.

During a long summer holiday, Harry Potter was stuck at 4 Privet Drive, where he unexpectedly encountered a Dementor attack. He was aware that Dumbledore and members of the Order of the Phoenix were intensifying their secret efforts to counter the growing power of Voldemort. However, no one was willing to share more details with him.

Frustrated and angry, Harry returned to Hogwarts, only to find that Dumbledore was avoiding him, and Hagrid was nowhere to be found. To make matters worse, Harry began to have recurring dreams of a long corridor. Every time he approached the door at the end of the corridor, he would wake up in excruciating pain, feeling as though a snake was writhing inside him. The shadow of a serpent grew increasingly vivid in Harry's mind, signaling Voldemort's encroaching presence. It was then that Dumbledore revealed a monumental secret to Harry.

As the new school year was about to begin, Professor Dumbledore unexpectedly arrived at the Dursleys' home to fetch Harry Potter, marking an unusual start to Harry's sixth year. Even more surprising events soon followed—Harry discovered an old Potions textbook in the classroom cupboard. Its previous owner, "The Half-Blood Prince," provided notes and instructions that turned Harry into a Potions prodigy.

Dumbledore began private lessons with Harry, guiding him through a series of harrowing memories about Voldemort’s youth, unraveling the mystery of his extraordinary origins. Together, Dumbledore and Harry set out to locate one of Voldemort’s Horcruxes, venturing into a distant cave where Dumbledore drank a mysterious poison to retrieve it.

Upon their return to Hogwarts, the Dark Mark loomed ominously above the castle. On the tower, Snape cast the Killing Curse at Dumbledore, sending him falling to his death as the phoenix sang a mournful requiem. After the funeral, Harry resolved to fulfill Dumbledore's final wish: to find and destroy the remaining Horcruxes.

With only four days until his seventeenth birthday, when he would officially become a wizard, Harry Potter was forced to leave 4 Privet Drive early, bidding farewell to the place he had called home for sixteen years.

The Order of the Phoenix devised a carefully planned operation to secretly relocate Harry, protecting him from Voldemort and his Death Eaters. However, their plans went awry when they were ambushed. Meanwhile, Voldemort had already seized control of Hogwarts, taken over the Ministry of Magic, and dominated half the wizarding world, plunging it into chaos. Accompanied by Ron and Hermione, Harry went into hiding, constantly on the run. While working to fulfill Dumbledore's final mission of destroying Voldemort's Horcruxes, Harry learned of the legendary Deathly Hallows and the possibility that possessing all three could guarantee Voldemort's defeat. However, Voldemort had also begun his hunt for the Elder Wand, sending his Death Eaters to track down Harry and set a deadly trap.
